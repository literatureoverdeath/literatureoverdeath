Literature Over Death

"I forgive everyone and I ask everyone for forgiveness. All right? Don't do too much gossip."

Cesare Pavese's poetics and conception of the world have been deeply influenced by his tormented human story. This intrinsic link implies that with the tragic triumph of the awareness of his own existential failure he stops writing and takes his own life.

This project, realized for the exam of Knowledge Representation and Representation of the course in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge, aims to reconstruct, through distant reading and close reading tools, the signature of one of the finest authors in capturing the most contrasting and painful aspects of human existence. The results obtained contribute and take concrete form in the constitution of a complex “Pavesian” Ontology.

We have analyzed one of his most famous collections, "Death will come and shall have your eyes" written during the last troubled years of his life and published posthumously, alongside, for completeness, his diary production (This Business Of Leaving), focusing especially on the last year before his suicide.

To further enrich the analysis and broaden its scope, we have enriched the corpus with balanced subcorpora by two other authors who with Pavese shared some characteristics of life and poetics and above all the deep connection with the concept of death: Sylvia Plath and Virginia Woolf.

Analyzed Topics

Biographical Analysis

An analysis of the lives of our chosen authors, in order to collect and define a global overview aimed at identifying the connections between personal experiences and literary production.

Rethorical Analysis

The analysis of the main rhetorical figures found in the latest compositions by Cesare Pavese and in a sample of poems by Sylvia Plath and Virginia Woolf. Each of the poems is accompanied by their respective critical analysis

Sentiment Analysis

Sentiment Analysis generally aims at classifying polarity toward a specific subjective/affective topic or question. The model on which we based the analysis was trained on tweets about anxiety and depression.

Emotion Detection

Emotion detection is the process identification of basic feelings. We took into consideration only the two most present emotions in the compositions analyzed and then, the relationship between the text sample and the extracted emotion is represented in the ontology through two sub-properties: hasPrimaryEmotion and hasSecondaryEmotion.

Ontology

At the end of our research work, we would like to develop an ontology aimed at identifying and defining Pavese's "signature", with a view to a subsequent evaluation of the affinities and differences between our three authors.
