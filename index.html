Literature Over Death

"I forgive everyone and I ask everyone for forgiveness. All right? Don't do too much gossip."

Cesare Pavese's poetics and conception of the world have been deeply influenced by his tormented human story. This intrinsic link implies that with the tragic triumph of the awareness of his own existential failure he stops writing and takes his own life. How is the act of committing suicide linked to the deconstruction of the poetic self? How does this feeling of inadequacy evolve and how is it reflected and translated in the author's prosistics and poetics? This project aims to try to answer these questions and try to reconstruct a signature of one of the finest authors in capturing the most contrasting and painful aspects of human existence. We have analyzed one of his most famous collections "Death will come and your eyes will have" written during the last troubled years of his life and published posthumously, alongside, for completeness, the last two years of diary production. To further enrich the analysis and broaden its scope, we have enriched the corpus with two balanced subcorpora by two other authors who with Pavese shared some characteristics of life and poetics and above all the way in which to get out of it, i.e. by committing suicide: Sylvia Plath and Emily Dickinson.

Analyzed Topics

Biographical Analysis

Some information about the lives of our chosen authors, in order to collect and define a global overview aimed at identifying the connections between personal experiences and literary production. The resulting information will be inserted in a subsection of the ontology

Rethorical Analysis

Manually realized analysis of the most recurrent poetical and rethorical tools adopted by the authors in their productions. Our attempt is to find a leitmotiv that links the three analyzed authors in order to demonstrate the underlying affinity between them.

Critics opinion

In this section we propose some points of view of accomplished critics who worked on the selected authors in a broad sense: we are interested in presenting their ideas not only related to the literary productions, but also to the human side of the writers, in order to find possible connections both between personal life and production of a single author and between the three authors themeselves.

Most Recurrent Words and Expressions

Through distant reading tools we aim at finding out the most frequent words and expressions (2 words, 3 words) in each of the 3 included subcorpora, a subcorpus being a sample of representative diary pages (prose) and poems of each author. Moreover we would like to perform the same type of analysis on the overall corpus so to highlight points of affinity and divergency.

Sentiment Analysis

We exploit sentiment analysis tools in order to automatically extract information on the polarity of samples of text at different levels of complexity: starting from a single verse (poems) or quote (diaries) to an overall analysis of subcorpora and the corpus in its globality. A secondary purpose is to highlight different grades of applicability of distant reading tools to poetry rather than prose.

Emotion Detection

We plan to use this branch of sentiment analysis to extract the most recurrent moods in the literary production of each author (highlighting possible differences between diaries and poems). As in the other sections we will then include a global analysis on the most recurrent emotions of the three subcorpora and the entire corpus.

Topic Modeling

We will exploit this statistical model to extract the hidden semantic structure of every text, subcorpora and global corpus.

Ontology

At the end of our research work, we would like to develop an ontology aimed at identifying and defining Pavese's "signature", with a view to a subsequent evaluation of the affinities and differences between our three authors.

Interactive Extra Tool

We would like to add a final possibility for the user to interact with ours subcorpora and deeply perceive the degree of semantic affinity between them. After a structural division of the texts and a grammatical and semantical tagging, the purpose is to allow an automatic recombination of literary extracts so to create a new coherent poem.
