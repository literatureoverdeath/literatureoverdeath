Rethorical Analysis

Manually realized analysis of the most recurrent poetical and rethorical tools adopted by the authors in their productions. Our attempt is to find a leitmotiv that links the three analyzed authors in order to demonstrate the underlying affinity between them.

Red earth, black earth

Red earth, black earth,

you come from the sea,

from the green burnt oil

whith ancient words

and desperate striving

and flowers among the rocks -

You don't know the speech

and striving of the sea you carry,

as rich as memory

like the bare countryside,

you, like a hard and

deep-sweet word,

old in the blood

that gathers in the eyes:

fresh, like fruit

that is memory and season -

you hold your breath

beneath the August sky,

the olives of your glance

can calm the sea,

and you live and live again

without surprise, as firm

as the earth, as dark

as the earth,olive-press

of seasons and dreams

exposed to the moon,

old indeed like

your mother's hands,

the bowl of the brazier.

1945, October, 27

You are like land

You are like land

that no one has ever talked of.

You expect nothing,

flowing out from the depths

like fruit among the branches.

A wind reaches you.

Dry, twice-dead things

obstruct you and blow away with the wind.

Ancient limbs and words.

You quiver in summer-time.

1945, October, 29

You are also a hill

You are also a hill

and a pebble pathway

and a game in the tame-fields,

and you know the vine

which is silent at night.

You say not a word.

There’s a silent land,

not your land.

There’s a silence which stays

over trees and hills.

There are landscapes and water.

You are a closed silence

which never gives way, you are lips

and dark eyes. You are the vine.

There’s a land waiting,

it says no word.

Days have passed

beneath burning skies.

You played with the clouds.

It’s poor land —

and your face knows it.

This too is the vine.

I’ll find the clouds

and the cane-brake again, the voices

like a moonlight shadow.

I'll find words again

beyond the brief living

of’night-time games

and butnt-out childhood.

Silence will be sweet.

You are earth and vine.

A silent flame

will burn up the land

like bonfires in the evening.

1945, October, 30-31

Your face is carved from stone

Your face is carved from stone,

blood from the hard earth,

you came from the sea.

You take all things

and look, and reject them

like the sea. In your heart

you have silence, you swallow

words. You are darkness.

For you dawn is silence.

You are like the voices

of the earth — the clang

of the bucket in the well,

the song of the fire,

the plop of an apple;

the hopeless, muttered

words at the door,

a child's cry - things

that never alter

You do not change. You are darkness.

You are the closed tavern,

with the bare earth floor,

which the boy once entered

when he had no shoes,

and always remembers.

You are the dark room

he always remembers.

Like the ancient courtyard

where the dawn began.
